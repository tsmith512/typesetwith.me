Typeface

The typeface, or font family, is a collection of individual fonts, each with a specific weight (light, medium, bold), width (condensed, normal, expanded), italicization, or other variant. For example, “Helvetica” is a typeface, but “12pt Helvetica Condensed Medium” is a font. Different typefaces must be set at different sizes or leading for better legibility.

“Display” typefaces, like Lobster, are designed to be used for decorative effect at larger sizes, particularly in headings. Avoid setting lengthy copy in display faces, because they can be difficult to read, even at appropriate sizes, for long periods.
