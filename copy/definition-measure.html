Measure

The Measure is the length of a line of text, measured in average characters per line. For a single-column design, the measure should ideally lie between 40 & 80 characters. Many typographers consider the perfect measure to be 65 characters. If the lines are too short, the text becomes disjointed. If lines are too long, the content loses rhythm as the reader searches for the start of each line. This is effect is amplified with improper leading. Generally, if the measure is wide, the leading of a text should be increased—if the measure is short, it can be decreased more safely.

(Adapted from Wikipedia)

Please note: the measure of samples in this application are approximated by diving the total number of characters by .5 minus the number of lines in the first paragraph of the sample.
