Measure

Measure (or sometimes “The Measure”) in typography is the length of a line of text. For a single-column design measure should ideally lie between 40 & 80 characters. Many typographers consider the perfect measure to be 65 characters. If the lines are too short then the text becomes disjointed, if they are too long the content loses rhythm as the reader searches for the start of each line. In print, reverse text (white text on black) also requires more leading.
