Contrast

Both in print and on-screen, using a softer alternative to white or black text can ease the strain of reading long passages. Additionally, when combining slightly grey text with stronger black headers, the visual hierarchy of the composition is strengthened.

On the flip side, it's important to retain sufficient contrast for accessibility, particularly if your project must meet certain guidelines. In this example, the slider turns orange when the text no longer meets WCAG AAA contrast guidelines, and red when the text no longer meets WCAG AA guidelines*. Learn more and test your own color combinations at WebAIM's Color Contrast Checker.

WCAG 2.0 level AA requires a contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text. Level AAA requires a contrast ratio of 7:1 for normal text and 4.5:1 for large text. Large text is defined as 14 point (typically 18.66px) and bold or larger, or 18 point (typically 24px) or larger.
— WebAIM

* Contrast recommendation here is "normal text."
