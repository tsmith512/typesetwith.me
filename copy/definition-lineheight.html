Line Height

The height of a line of text, measured from the baseline of one line to the baseline of the next. Line height may be specified as a multiple of the type size (as it is here), or using an actual measurement like points or pixels (frequently called leading).

Text with no spacing between lines appears cramped, making it difficult for the eye to track from the end of one line to the beginning of the next, and making vertical rivers between words more obvious than the spacing between lines. However, when the line height leaves too much space between text, lines can also be difficult to track visually, and vertical space is wasted.
